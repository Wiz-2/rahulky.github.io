About

Short academic profile

I am a PhD student in Machine Learning at IIT Bombay, advised by Prof. Arjun Nitin Bhagoji and Prof. Parthe Pandit. My research interests include adversarial robustness, kernel methods, random feature models, and statistical learning theory.

I am particularly interested in understanding how kernel machines compare with neural networks under strong adversarial evaluation, and in building theoretically grounded, empirically reliable machine learning systems.

Research

Current directions and projects

Adversarial Robustness in Kernel Methods and Neural Networks

2025 — Present
  • Studying robustness of kernel machines compared to deep neural networks under strong adversarial attacks such as PGD, AutoAttack, and SPSA.
  • Investigating double descent and robust generalization gaps in overparameterized regimes.

Neural Feature Ansatz in Adversarial Training

  • Analyzing the validity of neural feature ansatz for adversarially trained fully connected neural networks.
